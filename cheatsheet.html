Private Label Cheatsheet

Everything you need to launch your first Amazon private label product, condensed into one page. Save it, print it, use it.

01
Research

Product Research Checklist

  • Selling price between $25 and $65
  • Product weighs under 3 lbs
  • Not dominated by recognizable brands
  • Year-round demand confirmed on Google Trends
  • Top sellers have under 300 reviews
  • Simple to manufacture, no electronics or moving parts
  • Clear differentiation angle found in competitor reviews
  • 30%+ net margin confirmed in FBA Revenue Calculator
Tool: Helium 10 Black Box. Set min revenue $5K, max 300 reviews, price $20–$65.
02
Sourcing

Supplier Checklist

  • Contact 8–10 suppliers minimum on Alibaba
  • Filter by Verified Supplier and Trade Assurance only
  • Request pricing at 100, 300, and 500 unit MOQs
  • Ask about custom logo and custom packaging options
  • Request supplier handles FBA prep (labeling, poly bagging)
  • Negotiate payment: 30% deposit, 70% before shipment
  • Order samples from top 3 suppliers before any bulk order
  • Use Alibaba Trade Assurance for payment protection
First order: Start at 200–300 units. Tell them you plan to scale once the product sells.
03
Listing

Listing Optimization

  • Title: Brand + Primary Keyword + Feature + Size, under 200 chars
  • Primary keyword appears in the first 60 characters
  • 5 bullets: each starts with a CAPITALIZED benefit
  • Fill all 7–9 image slots — never leave any empty
  • Main image: white background, product fills 85% of frame
  • Include lifestyle photo, infographic, and size reference
  • Fill all 250 bytes of backend search terms (no repeats)
  • A+ Content live before launch (requires Brand Registry)
Target: Conversion rate above 10%. Under 8% means your main image or bullets need work.
04
Advertising

Launch PPC Setup

  • Auto campaign: $40/day, $1.25 default bid
  • Manual Broad campaign: $30/day, top 15 keywords
  • Competitor Targeting: $20/day, target top 5 ASINs
  • Run all three for 14 days before making changes
  • Download Search Term Report after 14 days
  • Move converting terms to a Manual Exact campaign
  • Negative match: 10+ clicks, zero sales
  • Target ACoS: 30–40% during launch phase
Mindset: Launch month you will not be profitable. You are buying data and ranking. The ROI comes in month 2 and 3.
05
Reviews

Getting Your First Reviews

  • Enroll in Amazon Vine via Brand Registry (up to 30 units)
  • Click Request a Review on every single order in Seller Central
  • Automate requests using Helium 10 Follow-Up
  • Include insert card with QR code to your review page
  • Ask genuine contacts to purchase and review honestly
  • Never offer incentives for reviews — immediate TOS violation
  • Respond to every negative review within 48 hours, professionally
Target: 20 genuine reviews in your first 60 days. That is where the conversion flywheel starts turning.
06
Brand

Brand Setup Checklist

  • 1–2 word brand name with .com domain available
  • Check USPTO trademark database before committing to a name
  • Logo in PNG transparent, SVG, light and dark versions
  • 2–3 brand colors with HEX and Pantone codes documented
  • File trademark at USPTO — pending status is enough for Brand Registry
  • Enroll in Amazon Brand Registry with pending trademark number
  • Design insert card with QR code and genuine thank-you message
Priority: File trademark on day one. Brand Registry unlocks A+ Content, Sponsored Brand ads, and hijacker protection.
07
Tools

Essential Tools

  • Helium 10 — product research, keywords, PPC optimization
  • Keepa — free price history and BSR tracking
  • Google Trends — free seasonality and demand check
  • Alibaba — supplier sourcing and outreach
  • Canva — packaging design, inserts, social content
  • Flexport / Freightos — freight forwarding quotes
  • MailerLite — email list, free up to 1,000 subscribers
  • FBA Revenue Calculator — always check margins before sourcing
Start free: Helium 10, Keepa, Google Trends, and Canva all have free tiers. Do not pay for tools before you have a validated product.
08
Avoid These

Common First-Timer Mistakes

  • Choosing a product priced under $20 or over $70
  • Skipping the sample order to save $150
  • Launching before the listing is fully optimized
  • Running out of stock mid-launch — ranking tanks overnight
  • Ignoring negative reviews instead of responding to them
  • Paying 100% upfront to a first-time supplier
  • Rushing product research — spend at least 2 full weeks here
  • Not filing a trademark before or immediately after launch
The real mistake: Rushing. Every shortcut costs significantly more to fix than the time it saved.
